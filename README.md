# Project for Accessible Radio Telescopes (PART)

## About Us

We are **Narayan Dwan-Holland, Aliana He, Kevin Fan, Emma Enyu Zhang, and Yanfu Fan** — students from Narrabundah College, ACT, and participants of the Science Mentors ACT program.

## Our Mission

PART is our initiative to design, manufacture, and distribute affordable radio telescopes for rural educators and students. We aim to produce a simple, reliable telescope capable of recording signals at the **21-cm hydrogen line**, with a total build cost under **$500.00**.

Our design uses a commercially available weather satellite dish, a conductive plastic base, low-noise amplifiers, bandpass filters, a software-defined radio, and a motor system. We plan to manufacture **25 telescopes** and distribute them freely to rural high schools and colleges.

We believe that improving access to scientific instruments can help close the education gap between urban and rural Australian students, and that community-distributed radio telescopes can contribute meaningful data to astronomical research.
